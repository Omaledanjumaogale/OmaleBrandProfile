◆ The Problem

A business without records is
invisible to opportunity.

Nigeria has 41.5 million businesses, a GDP of $477 billion, and chartered accountants across every state. Yet most entrepreneurs can't access basic financial services. Here's why that's costing Nigeria's future.

Loan Applications Declined

Banks demand audited financial statements. Without them, 8 in 10 Nigerian SME loan applications fail — not because the business isn't viable, but because it can't be seen.

FIRS Letters No One Can Read

The Federal Inland Revenue Service sends compliance notices to millions of unregistered businesses. Without an accountant to interpret them, entrepreneurs face fines, penalties — or worse.

The ₦5M/Year Accountant Wall

Big-4 accounting firms charge ₦3–8 million per year. For the entrepreneur in Alaba Market, the startup founder in Yaba, or the NGO director in Enugu — that wall has always existed. Until now.

500 Qualified Auditors With No Platform

Across Nigeria, ICAN, ACCA, and ANAN certified professionals have the skills — but no national marketplace to find clients, verify their work, and scale their income. Their talent is invisible too.
