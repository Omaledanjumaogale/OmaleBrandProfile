The Crisis We're Solving

Nigeria's Healthcare System
Is Broken in Six Ways at Once.

These are not abstract statistics. They are the daily reality of 220 million people — including you, your mother, your child.

👨‍⚕️
Doctor Scarcity
Nigeria has 0.4 doctors per 1,000 people. The WHO recommends 10×. Qualified doctors leave for Canada and the UK at 5,000+ per year.
0.4 per 1,000 people
🏥
No Access, No Time
The average Nigerian travels 30–90 minutes to reach a hospital. In emergencies, every minute is a life-or-death variable no one should have to gamble with.
90 min avg travel time
💊
Counterfeit Drugs
30% of drugs in Nigerian circulation are unverified or counterfeit. Patients are paying real money for pills that may make them sicker.
30% unverified supply
💸
Out-of-Pocket Crisis
70% of Nigerians pay for healthcare entirely out of pocket, with no insurance, no savings structure, and no safety net. One illness bankrupts families.
70% uninsured
🎓
Unemployed Professionals
400,000 trained Nigerian healthcare professionals are under-employed or unemployed — skills wasted while patients suffer without care.
400K under-employed
🏃
Medical Tourism Drain
$2 billion leaves Nigeria annually as patients fly to India, the UK and Ghana for care that could and should be delivered here. That wealth must stay home.
$2B annual loss
